Project Overview

Understanding the vision behind Self Nurture and its mission to make mental health support accessible to everyone

What is Self Nurture?

Self-nurture refers to the practices, habits, and activities that individuals engage in to care for their emotional, mental, and sometimes physical well-being.

Life can be overwhelming, and we all need support sometimes. That's why we created Self Nurture‚Äî a mental health app designed to help you navigate stress, anxiety, depression, burnout, and loneliness in a way that feels natural and supportive.

Our Mission

We believe mental well-being should be accessible to everyone, so we've made it easy for you to track your mood, explore guided exercises, and connect with a like-minded community‚Äîall at your own pace.

Whether you're looking for simple self-care tips or deeper emotional support, Self Nurture is here to remind you that you're not alone. Your mental health matters, and we're here to help you take care of it, one step at a time.

Holistic Approach

The app offers a holistic approach by integrating self-care tools and professional resources, while emphasizing inclusivity for underserved groups like transgender individuals. By utilizing insights from user surveys, the app delivers personalized recommendations, empowering users to manage stress, anxiety, depression, sleep issues, and more.

02

Problem & Solution

Identifying the challenges and crafting meaningful solutions

Problem Statement

Mental health issues are increasingly prevalent, yet individuals face significant barriers such as:

  • Limited accessibility to mental health resources
  • Social stigma around seeking help
  • Lack of personalized care options
  • Gaps in support for diverse demographics
  • Difficulty connecting fitness and mental health

Existing mental health solutions often fail to address the specific needs of diverse demographics, leaving gaps in support, particularly for underserved groups like transgender individuals.

Solution Statement

The app offers a holistic approach by:

  • Integrating self-care tools and professional resources
  • Emphasizing inclusivity for underserved groups
  • Delivering personalized recommendations through user surveys
  • Empowering users to manage stress, anxiety, and depression
  • Providing sleep tracking and improvement tips
  • Connecting users with mental health professionals

Research

Deep dive into user needs and market analysis

Research Objectives

  • Understand User Needs: Identify prevalent mental health issues and coping strategies among users
  • User Interaction: Examine how users interact with mental health resources and their expectations from a digital app
  • Feature Validation: Confirm that app features meet genuine user needs through data insights
  • Competitive Analysis: Assess current mental health apps to uncover unmet needs and improvement areas

Scope of Work

  • UI/UX Research
  • User Testing
  • User Interviews
  • Business Analysis
  • Competitor Analysis
  • User Stories & User Flow
  • Moodboard & Concept
  • A/B Testing
  • Style Guide & App Interface
  • Final Design

Research Questions

  • What are the thoughts and actions of people experiencing depression?
  • What are the primary user needs?
  • What pain points are users facing?
  • How are users currently overcoming difficulties?

Interview Questions

  • What's your mood lately? How long has this been going on?
  • What makes your mood change?
  • When you feel stressed, what do you usually do?
  • What do you think will make you better?
  • Have you ever been to a psychiatrist?
  • Do you think you need outside support?
